History:

Sadberry Intermediate School, located in Bryan, Texas, was named in honor of O.W. Sadberry Sr., a trailblazing educator and community leader who profoundly influenced education and civil rights in the region. O.W. Sadberry Sr. served as the principal of Booker T. Washington Elementary School, which was historically significant as a school for African American students during segregation. He was instrumental in integrating faculty and preparing for student desegregation in the late 1960s. Beyond his work in education, he supported military families, including the Tuskegee Airmen stationed in Bryan, by helping them secure housing during an era of widespread racial segregationâ€‹

The idea to name the school after Sadberry was championed by his son, Oliver Wayne Sadberry Jr., a community historian and curator at the Brazos Valley African American Museum. He worked with a dedicated committee to honor his father's legacy of service and education. The school officially opened in August 2023 as part of the Bryan Independent School District and currently serves grades 5 and 6. Its mission is rooted in continuing Sadberry's legacy of fostering opportunity, equity, and academic excellence for all students
